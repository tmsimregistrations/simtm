Mandatory Notice — Republic Act No. 11934

All SIM cards in the Philippines must be registered under RA 11934. Unregistered SIMs are permanently deactivated by the NTC deadline. Registration is 100% FREE. Anyone asking you to pay is running a scam.

Section 01

Why You Absolutely Must Register Your TM SIM

Republic Act No. 11934, signed into law in 2022 and enforced from 2023 onward, closes the anonymity loophole that SMS scammers exploited for years. Text scams, OTP interception schemes draining GCash accounts, fake parcel delivery notifications — all of these relied on unregistered SIMs.

Beyond the law, your TM number is the center of your digital life: GCash, Maya, online banking OTPs, Lazada, Shopee, SSS, Grab — all tied to your mobile number. Losing your number to deactivation means potentially losing access to all of them simultaneously.

Requirements: Valid IDs Accepted

🪪

Philippine National ID (PhilSys)

The gold standard. Near-zero rejection rate. Fastest processing — use this if you have it.

🚗

Driver's License (LTO)

Accepted for front and back upload. Ensure it is not expired. Make sure the OCR code on the back is legible.

✈️

Philippine Passport (DFA)

Excellent for OFWs registering abroad. Passport must not be expired. Works for online registration from any country.

🏛️

UMID Card (SSS/GSIS)

Widely held by employed Filipinos. Very reliable for portal upload. Both sides required.

📋

PRC Professional ID

For licensed professionals. Must show full legal name, photo, PRC seal, and must not be expired.

🗳️

Voter's ID / Comelec Cert

Physical Voter's ID accepted. Comelec certification also accepted with a secondary document.

📸
How to Photograph Your ID for Best Results

Place ID flat on a dark matte surface. Use natural window light — no flash. Show all four corners. File under 5MB in JPEG format. Blurry or glare-heavy photos account for ~60% of all failed registrations.

Section 04

Step-by-Step: Complete Online Registration

1️⃣

Prepare Everything First

Photograph your ID in good lighting. Confirm your TM SIM has cellular signal and can receive SMS. Have your full legal name and address from your ID ready.

2️⃣

Open the Official Portal

Go to the official Globe registration website. Select "Touch Mobile (TM)." Type the URL directly — do not click links from unknown sources.

3️⃣

Enter TM Number & Get OTP

Type your 11-digit TM number (starts with 09). Click "Send OTP." Enter the 6-digit code within 5 minutes. Max 3 OTP requests per session.

4️⃣

Fill Personal Information

Enter details exactly as on your ID — not your nickname. Required: full name, middle name, birthdate, complete address, nationality, civil status.

5️⃣

Choose ID Type & Upload

Select your ID type from the dropdown. Upload front (and back for Driver's License and UMID). Keep file under 5MB. JPEG is more reliable than PNG.

6️⃣

Capture Live Selfie

Allow camera access. Remove glasses, hats, face masks. Face a light source. Hold phone steady at eye level. Look directly at camera. The AI compares your face to your ID photo.

7️⃣

Review All Details & Submit

Before submitting: verify name spelling, birthdate (MM/DD/YYYY), and complete address. A wrong birthdate means you'll need to revisit a Globe Store to correct it.

8️⃣

Save Your Reference Number

Screenshot your Reference Number immediately and save it in multiple places. It is required for SIM replacement and cannot be recovered if lost.

What Happens After Submission?

Your registration enters TM/Globe's verification queue. Most registrations are confirmed within 2–24 hours. You'll receive a confirmation SMS. Your SIM continues to work normally during review.

Confirmation SMS: "Your TM SIM registration has been successfully processed. Reference Number: [XXXXXXXXXX]"
Section 05

Troubleshooting: Fix Common Registration Errors

📵

OTP Not Received

Toggle Airplane Mode off/on. Restart your phone. Remove and reinsert SIM. Wait 3–5 minutes then click Resend. If persistent, try the USSD method or visit a Globe Store.

🖼️

ID Photo Upload Failed

Retake in natural daylight on a dark surface. Ensure all four edges are visible. Compress to under 5MB. Convert PNG to JPEG. Avoid scanning apps that add watermarks.

❌

Name Mismatch Error

Your name must match your ID letter-for-letter — including spaces, hyphens, ñ, and suffixes. If ID shows "DELA CRUZ" as two words, enter it exactly that way.

🔄

"This SIM is Already Registered"

The SIM may have been pre-registered by the retailer. Call TM hotline 211 with proof of purchase to request a SIM registration reset.

⏳

Portal Freezes or Won't Load

Clear browser cache and cookies. Switch to Chrome. Disable VPNs. Avoid 12PM–3PM peak hours. Try again after 6PM or the following morning.

🤳

Selfie Not Accepted

Full face must be centered and clearly visible. Remove accessories. Face a light source in front of you — backlit selfies fail. Hold phone steady at eye level.

Section 06

Lost or Stolen SIM: Emergency Protocol

🚨
Act Immediately — Every Minute Counts

Call 211 from any TM or Globe phone the moment you realize your SIM is lost or stolen. SIM blocking takes minutes and is free 24/7. Delaying an hour gives criminals time to drain linked financial accounts via your OTPs.

1

Block Your SIM Immediately — Call 211

Call 211 (free, 24/7) from any TM or Globe phone. From other networks, call (02) 7730-1000. Provide your TM number and identity verification details. Get the case reference number before hanging up.

2

Secure Your Financial Accounts

On another device, immediately change passwords on GCash, Maya, banking apps, and email. Call your bank's hotline to temporarily freeze internet banking. Check GCash transaction history for unauthorized transfers.

3

Gather Documents for SIM Replacement

Required: valid government ID, SIM Registration Reference Number, and ideally an Affidavit of Loss from a notary public. A police blotter further strengthens your claim if the SIM was stolen.

4

Visit Globe Store for Replacement SIM

Bring documents to any authorized Globe Store. Upon successful identity verification, they issue a replacement SIM with your same TM number. Load balance, active promos, and Easy Plan 150 subscriptions all transfer. Replacement fee: ₱40–₱100.

Section 07

Frequently Asked Questions

How many TM SIM cards can I register?
+
Under RA 11934, you can register a maximum of three (3) SIM cards in total across ALL mobile networks combined — not three per network. Intentionally circumventing this limit is a criminal offense under the law.
Is there a registration deadline in 2026?
+
Yes, and it is a rolling deadline. Newly purchased SIMs must be registered within 180 days of purchase. The NTC actively deactivates unregistered SIMs that have passed their individual deadlines. If yours is unregistered, it is at immediate risk.
I'm an OFW abroad. Can I register from overseas?
+
Yes, provided your SIM is on international roaming and can receive the OTP SMS. Use your Philippine Passport as your primary ID. If roaming is not active, coordinate with a trusted person in the Philippines to assist.
How do I register a TM SIM for a minor?
+
Minors cannot register SIMs in their own name. A parent or legal guardian must register on the minor's behalf using their own valid government ID and a signed parental consent form available at Globe Stores.
What happens to my GCash and load if my SIM is deactivated?
+
Your remaining prepaid load balance is forfeited permanently with no refund. Active promo subscriptions also expire. For GCash, you will lose the ability to access it through normal OTP-based login and must contact GCash support separately with valid ID to recover wallet access.
My SIM registration shows "Pending Verification" — is that normal?
+
"Pending Verification" is completely normal. Your submission is in the queue for review. Most registrations confirm within 24–48 hours. IDs that are harder to verify may take up to 72 hours. Your SIM continues to work normally during this period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use Easy Plan 150 and another promo at the same time?
+
Yes — with a distinction. Easy Plan 150 is fully compatible with data add-ons and time-based promos (Night Owl, Weekend Unli). These stack on top of its benefits. However, you cannot have two Easy Plan 150 subscriptions active simultaneously. If you activate while one is running, the new one queues to begin after the current one expires.
Why can't I call Smart, DITO, or TNT numbers?
+
The unlimited voice call inclusion is specifically for TM-to-TM and TM-to-Globe calls. For unlimited calls to all networks including Smart and DITO, add UNLI ALLNET 249 (₱249 for 15 days) on top of your Easy Plan 150. The combination gives you complete coverage for ₱399 per month.
What speed do I get after my data runs out?
+
After your 6–8GB main allocation and 2GB entertainment bonus are consumed, data throttles to approximately 512Kbps — adequate for text messaging, basic email, Maps navigation, and GCash/Maya access (which are zero-rated anyway). Not suitable for video streaming or large downloads. Adding a data top-up restores full LTE/5G speed immediately.
Is there a lock-in period or minimum subscription requirement?
+
Absolutely not. Easy Plan 150 is a fully prepaid, no-contract subscription. Zero penalties or fees for not renewing. No minimum subscription period, no cancellation fee, no installment requirement.
How do I check how much data I have left?
+
Three ways: (1) Dial *143# → My Account → Data Remaining; (2) Open the GlobeOne app for real-time graphical progress bar; (3) Send DATA BAL to 8080 for an SMS breakdown. Checking via *143# works even with zero load balance and zero internet.

2

Check Registration Status

Send "REG STATUS" (or check via *143# → SIM Registration → Check Status) to verify your current registration status. You should see either: Registered, Pending Verification, or Unregistered. If Unregistered, go to our registration guide immediately.

SMS: REG STATUS → 5151
3

Stop Auto-Renewal

Send "STOP [PROMO KEYWORD]" to 8080 at least 24 hours before expiry to prevent auto-renewal. Example: send "STOP EASY150" to stop Easy Plan 150 from renewing. Your current subscription continues until its natural expiry.

SMS: STOP [KEYWORD] → 8080
4

Check Data Remaining

Dial *143# → My Account → Data Remaining. Or send "DATA BAL" to 8080. Or check the GlobeOne app dashboard for a real-time graphical view of your main data, entertainment bonus, and any data add-ons separately.

SMS: DATA BAL → 8080
Section 03

Common Issues & Fixes

My TM SIM has no signal — what do I do?
+
First, toggle Airplane Mode off and on. Then restart your phone completely. Remove and reinsert your SIM. If still no signal, try your SIM in another phone to rule out a phone hardware issue. Check @Globe_PH on Twitter/X for network outage advisories in your area. If no outage reported, call 211 from another phone to report the issue.
I was charged ₱150 but didn't receive promo confirmation — what happened?
+
This is most likely auto-renewal of Easy Plan 150. Check *143# → My Account → Active Subscriptions to verify. If a promo IS active and working, the deduction is correct. If no promo shows as active and you were charged, call 211 immediately with the date and time of the charge. Keep your load balance receipt or GCash transaction record as proof.
How do I transfer my TM number to another SIM (SIM swap)?
+
Visit any authorized Globe Store in person. Bring your valid government ID (the same type used for registration) and your SIM Registration Reference Number. If your original SIM is lost or damaged, an Affidavit of Loss may also be required. The store processes the SIM swap and issues you a new SIM with your same TM number, load balance, and active subscriptions.
My promo keyword SMS is returning "Insufficient Balance" but I have load — why?
+
Some promos require a slightly higher balance than the promo cost itself (e.g., ₱155 for a ₱150 promo) due to a small transaction buffer. Ensure you have at least ₱20 more than the promo cost. Also check if you have an existing active promo that conflicts with the new one — you may need to wait for it to expire first.
How long does a TM hotline call usually take?
+
Wait times vary. Simple inquiries (balance check, promo status) are faster using self-service (*143# or GlobeOne app). For hotline calls, expect 5–15 minutes for simple concerns and 20–45 minutes for complex issues like SIM blocking or registration disputes. Calling between 7AM–9AM or 9PM–11PM tends to have shorter wait times than midday or evening peak hours.
